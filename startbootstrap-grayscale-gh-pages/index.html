My Book Recommendations

A short list of my favorite books, and why you should read them.

Take a look

Why Books?

According to the Pew Research Center, 23% of American adults say that they have not read a book at least in part in the last year. And if your income is below $30,000 per year, you are more than twice as likely to not read a book in a year than your peers earning over $75,000. So why books? Reading, although sometimes boring, is a significant factor not only when it comes to income, but also a significant factor in education. Plus, if you read the right book, who knows, you might actually enjoy it.

Rich Dad Poor Dad

A classic by Robert Kiyosaki. A must read to anyone who doesn't want to be stressed about money. Kiyosaki teaches how the rich think about money.

12 rules for life

An antidote for Chaos

Famed Clinical Psychologist Jordan B. Peterson presents his 12 Rules for life, discussing discipline, freedom, adventure and responsibility, distilling the world's wisdom.

The Count of Monte Cristo

A fiction novel of treachery, love, vengeance, and forgiveness by French Romantic Author Alexandre Dumas. Witness young Edmond Dantes, as he is betrayed by his once thought comrades, and vows to take revenge on all those who falsely accused him and reward all those who were loyal to him.
